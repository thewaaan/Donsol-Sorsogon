Places, Activities, and Must-Try Foods

Must-Do Activities

Whale Shark Interaction (Butanding)

Swim alongside the gentle giants, commonly sighted from November to June. Peak season is typically February to May. Tours are strictly regulated by Butanding Interaction Officers (BIOs).

Firefly River Watching

Go on a magical boat ride along the Donsol River at night to see thousands of glowing lights in this firefly watching tour.

Diving and Snorkeling

Explore the vibrant marine life, including the chance to spot Manta Rays and soft corals. Dive spots are often around Ticao Island and the famous Manta Bowl.

Ticao Island Excursion

Take a boat trip to Ticao Island for more remote beaches and diving opportunities.

Must-Try Local Foods

Donsol, situated in Sorsogon, offers traditional Bicolano cuisine rich in coconut milk and chili.

Bicol Express

The ultimate Bicolano dish: tender pork simmered in a rich, spicy sauce made from coconut milk and chilies (sili).

Laing

A creamy vegetarian favorite featuring taro leaves (gabi) slowly stewed in thick coconut cream, often spiced with chilies and shrimp paste (bagoong).

Kinunot na Tapas/Pagi

A local delicacy of flaked fish (tapas) or stingray (pagi) mixed with coconut milk, moringa (malunggay) leaves, and chilies. It's often served as an appetizer.

Binuntong

A traditional Bicolano delicacy made from glutinous rice and coconut, wrapped in banana leaves. It can be eaten as is or with sweet additions like mangoes.

Timitim

A sweet native delicacy, this is a steamed cake made from grated cassava and coconut milk, often topped with sweet latik (coconut curd).

Pili Nuts

A Sorsogon specialty. These versatile nuts are sold candied, roasted, or as key ingredients in various local pastries and pasalubong (souvenirs).
