Discover Donsol

Where the earth meets the coast.

The Municipality of Donsol, located in the province of Sorsogon, is world-renowned for being one of the best places to experience whale shark (locally known as butanding) interactions. This vibrant coastal town combines its rich natural wonders with a warm, welcoming community. The local government is committed to sustainable tourism and environmental conservation, ensuring that Donsol remains a premier destination for both tourists and locals alike.

Donsol: History and Background

This was a mere barrio of Quipia (now Jovellar, in Albay province) until 1668 when Donsol became an independent parish. Donsol became a pueblo civil, or town, in 1800.

The present townsite of Donsol used to be known as the ancient village or sitio of Barra, which is located near the coast. By virtue of a petition by the Donsol townsfolk, who were then situated in the interior, which is now known as Banuang Gurang (old town), seeking the town's transfer to sitio Barra, because it would be, according to them, economically beneficial, the town was transferred to its present site on July 11, 1831. The move indeed proved to be most beneficial, with the ready availability of trading, transportation and communications in the new site, that Donsol so outstripped its former mother town Quipia that this became its mere visita until 1877 when Quipia, too, became a separate town under Albay, and renamed Jovellar.

Donsol got its name from “donsolan”. Bikol for anvil - that heavy metal block used by blacksmiths to fashion various metal implements, such as, machetes, knives, scissors, etc. According to ancient lore, Donsol was then inhabited by expert blacksmiths, which is why when one was looking for some place where one could get a machete or knife, one was invariably directed to go to the donsolan. Or go to Donsol. But Donsol is now famous worldwide, no longer for its metalworks, but for those awesome, but gentle, creatures known as Butandings, or whale sharks, that converge in large numbers at the mouth of the Donsol River during certain months of the year.

Beyond the Butanding: Other Attractions

While the whale sharks command international attention, Donsol offers a variety of attractions and experiences that allow visitors to explore the municipality's culture and natural beauty by land. For those interested in local history, the old municipal hall ruins and the Spanish-era church of St. Joseph provide a glimpse into the town's colonial past and architectural heritage. Food enthusiasts can indulge in authentic Bikolano cuisine, which is famous for its use of coconut milk and chili. Specialties like laing (taro leaves in coconut cream) and Bikol Express (pork stewed in coconut milk and chilies) are readily available and offer a fiery, flavorful culinary adventure. Furthermore, the extensive coastline features black sand beaches that are perfect for sunset viewing or a peaceful, uncrowded walk, offering a tranquil complement to the excitement of the whale shark interaction.

Local Culture and Economy

The local economy of Donsol has been dramatically transformed by the rise of whale shark tourism. What was once a primarily agricultural and fishing community has now embraced sustainable ecotourism, providing livelihood opportunities through guiding, accommodation, and related services. The culture remains deeply rooted in Bikolano traditions, with a focus on hospitality and community. This commitment to maintaining a close-knit feel, despite international recognition, contributes significantly to the town's charm. Visitors can enjoy fresh, locally sourced seafood and experience the warmth of the Donsol community, making the trip not just an adventure, but a genuine cultural exchange.
